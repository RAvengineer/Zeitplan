A Timetable Management web app for VITians.

What is Zeitplan?

Meaning:

Pronounced as tsait ¬∑ plaan, Zeitplan is a German word, that means Timetable.

It's purpose:

For all VITians, checking the timetable after each lecture is essential. Most commonly used method for the process is keeping a photograph of the timetable, and checking it after each class. But, this sounds tedious!

So, how about you get notified about the next lecture just when a lecture ends. With Google accounts being used world-wide, instead of creating an app - which would be like re-inventing the wheels, Google Calendar can be used for this purpose. And on top of that, Zeitplan won't ask you to add the slots and lectures manually!

All you have to do is to copy-paste the timetable from VTOP and provide few details about the events, and then all the lectures will be added to your Calendar Account as Events. Now, you will be able to see next lecture's details in your notification bar, provided you have Google Calendar app installed on your mobile. For more, you can use the Calendar Widget to get your lecture details on home screen in a more organised manner.

How to use Zeitplan?

How to add timetable?

  1. Click on Get Started on Homepage or Click on Add Events in ZCalendar dropdown
  2. *You will be redirected to Google Sign In page. Choose the account in which you want the lecture schedule(events) to be added.
  3. *A This app isn't verified page will be displayed. Click on Advanced and proceed to Go to pythonanywhere.com (unsafe).
  4. *A Grant pythonanywhere.com permission popup will be displayed. Click on Allow. Click on Allow again to Confirm your choices.
  5. Paste the Raw timetable from VTOP in the respective section.(Demo available on Homepage)
  6. Customize the Event parameters
  7. Click on Add Events

* For first-time users only.


How to use a different Google account?

  1. Click on the Delete Cookies button in the ZCalendar dropdown.
  2. Follow the How to add timetable?

Messed up while adding Events?

  1. Go to your Goggle Calendar account.
  2. In the top right, click Settings Settings.
  3. In the left column, find the Secondary Calendar named Zeitplan.
  4. Click the name of the calendar.
  5. Click Remove calendar Delete Delete permanently.
  6. Now, you are ready to create a fresh calendar with correct timetable.

PS:

I know See, edit, share, and permanently delete all the calendars you can access using Google Calendar sounds scary! To make it clear, since Zeitplan has no database, all the data related to your account are stored on your local machine as cookies. You delete the cookies and the access is gone. You can delete the cookies by clicking on Delete Cookies in ZCalendar dropdown.
